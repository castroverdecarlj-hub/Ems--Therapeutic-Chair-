Electrical Muscle Stimulation Therapeutic Chair

"What if a simple chair could help restore strength, comfort, and independence for elders and persons with disabilities?"

About the Project

The Electrical Muscle Stimulation (EMS) Therapeutic Chair is a prototype designed to support the physical wellness of elderly individuals and Persons With Disabilities (PWDs). This chair uses electrical muscle stimulation technology to activate muscles, improve circulation, and reduce discomfort while the user is seated comfortably.

Parts of the Chair

Comfortable Seat and Backrest – designed to support the body and allow the user to relax during therapy.

EMS Stimulation System – provides electrical muscle stimulation through different therapy modes that target muscles.

Electric Stimulation Modes

Beat Mode – Provides rhythmic pulses that help stimulate muscles and improve blood circulation.
Massage Mode – Simulates a gentle massage that helps relax muscles and reduce tension.
Activation Mode – Helps activate weak or inactive muscles by sending controlled electrical signals.
Train Mode – Designed to help strengthen muscles through repeated stimulation patterns.
Knead Mode – Mimics kneading motions similar to therapeutic massage.
Shaping Mode – Helps improve muscle tone and muscle conditioning.
Extrusion Mode – Applies deeper stimulation for muscle relaxation and recovery.
Slap Mode – Produces quick pulses that energize and stimulate muscle movement.

The chair has adjustable intensity levels from Level 1 (minimum) to Level 19 (maximum), allowing users to choose the stimulation strength depending on their muscle needs.

How It Works

The EMS system sends safe electrical signals to specific muscle areas through pads placed inside the chair. These signals cause the muscles to contract and relax, similar to natural muscle movement. This process helps improve muscle activity, reduce stiffness, and promote better circulation.

Benefits for Persons with Disabilities (PWDs)

The EMS Therapeutic Chair can help PWDs maintain muscle activity even when mobility is limited. It may support muscle stimulation, improve circulation, reduce stiffness, and help users feel more comfortable during long periods of sitting.

Benefits for Elderly Individuals

For elders, the chair helps maintain muscle strength, reduce body pain, improve relaxation, and support overall physical wellness. Regular use may also help reduce muscle weakness caused by aging.
